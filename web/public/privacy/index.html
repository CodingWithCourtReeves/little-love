Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Coding with Court LLC (“LittleLove,” “we,” “us”) makes a private messenger for couples. This policy explains what we collect, what we deliberately cannot see, and who we work with. LittleLove is for adults: you must be 18 or older to use it.

The short version

  • Your messages, calls, voice notes, photos, and video are end-to-end encrypted. We store only ciphertext we have no key to read.
  • We don’t use third-party trackers, analytics, advertising, or AI. We keep only content-free crash diagnostics on our own servers to fix bugs. Crash reporting from the app is off unless you turn it on, and we don’t sell or share your data.
  • We don’t track who you talk to or when for any purpose beyond delivering your messages.
  • We hold the minimum we need to run the service (your public keys, your partner link, a push token, routing timestamps).
  • Questions or requests: privacy@littlelove.dev.

What we can’t see

LittleLove is end-to-end encrypted. Your messages and shared files are encrypted on your device and addressed to your partner’s device key, using keys that live only on your devices (stored in the iOS Keychain). The content travels through our server as ciphertext we have no key to open, and is delivered to your partner’s device, where it is decrypted. Calls are peer-to-peer and encrypted in transit. Your profile display name and avatar are also end-to-end encrypted.

Because of this design, we cannot read your messages, listen to your calls, or view your photos and video. We also cannot recover them for you if you lose access to your device and recovery phrase.

What we collect and store

Account and identity

  • Your public encryption keys (these are public by design; your private keys never leave your device).
  • An account identifier and your chosen handle.
  • Your partner link (which two accounts are connected). LittleLove pairs exactly one partner per account.

Operating the service

  • Routing information and timestamps needed to deliver and order messages.
  • A device push-notification token, so we can tell your device a message has arrived. Our push notifications are content-free, and never contain message text or sender details.
  • Connection information such as IP address, used transiently to operate the service and guard against abuse.

If you contact us

  • The email address and message you send through our contact form or to privacy@littlelove.dev, so we can reply.

What we don’t do

We don’t use third-party trackers, analytics, advertising, or AI. The only diagnostics we keep are our own content-free crash reports, described under Crash diagnostics below. We verified this in our own source code, which is public. We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information, and we do not build advertising or behavioral profiles.

Crash diagnostics

To find and fix bugs, we keep crash and error diagnostics on infrastructure we run ourselves, never a third-party analytics service and never sentry.io. There are two kinds, and neither ever includes the content of your messages, calls, or files:

  • From our server, automatically. Our server only ever sees your content encrypted, so its diagnostics cannot contain message content by design. We also strip identifiers, such as handles and account ids, before a report is stored. You can ask us to turn yours off by emailing us.
  • From the app on your device, only if you turn it on. This is off by default. Your device is the one place your messages are readable, so before any report leaves your phone we remove message text, contact and display names, file paths, and identifiers, and send only technical details about what went wrong, such as the error and where in the code it happened. You can switch this on or off anytime in the app under Profile.

Service providers

We rely on a small number of providers to run LittleLove. None of them receive the plaintext content of your messages, calls, or files:

  • Apple: delivers our content-free push notifications (Apple receives a device token and that a message arrived, never its content).
  • Cloudflare: provides connection credentials for calls (the call media is encrypted end-to-end and flows peer-to-peer), encrypted file storage (we store only ciphertext), and DNS/hosting for this website.
  • Railway: hosts our application server and database, which hold only the data described above (including ciphertext, never keys to it).
  • Resend: delivers the email when you write to us through the contact form.

How we use information

We use the information above only to operate, secure, and support LittleLove: to deliver your messages and calls, notify your device, prevent abuse, and respond when you contact us.

Retention and your choices

You can delete your account at any time from within the app, or by emailing privacy@littlelove.dev. Deleting your account removes your account record, public keys, partner link, and stored ciphertext from our servers. Because your content is end-to-end encrypted, deletion is irreversible and we cannot restore it.

Depending on where you live (for example, California and certain other US states), you may have rights to access or delete the personal information we hold about you. Email us and we’ll honor applicable requests.

Security

Message content is end-to-end encrypted, your private keys are stored in the iOS Keychain on your device, and all connections to our servers are encrypted in transit. No system is perfect, but our design minimizes what could ever be exposed: the server simply does not hold the keys to your conversations.

Age requirement

LittleLove is intended only for people who are 18 or older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe someone under 18 has created an account, contact privacy@littlelove.dev and we’ll address it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as LittleLove evolves. We’ll change the “last updated” date above, and for material changes we’ll do our best to let you know in the app.
